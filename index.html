I began conducting research with Dr. Cliff Johnson at Northwestern University and CIERA, and Dr. Anil Seth at the University of Utah on two projects which you can read about below. My current project is working with Dr. Gail Zasowski at the University of Utah.

Star Clusters in M33

Our main goal was to construct a catalog of Star Clusters in the Triangulum Galaxy(M33) using data from the Hubble Space Telescope. We used the Citizen Science platform hosted by zooniverse.org to conduct a visual search for star clusters. Because star clusters are a key tool to studying star formation at different epochs, this catalog will be the basis of gaining usefull information about one of our nearest galactic neighbors.

This star cluster catalog was the basis for my next project, measuring and analysing the star cluster mass function of M33. The star cluster mass function provides valuable insights into how large star cluster can be in a galaxy, which we found to be consistent with the local star forming rates. In addition, we analyzed how impactful individual star cluster mass uncertainties are in measuring the cluster mass function.
